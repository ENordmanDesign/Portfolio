Winery Website

UI/UX Design

Project Scope

Objective

Re-design an existing website for a family winery in California. Take existing written content and add updated photos and content to a website with a fresh design and more integrated features. Solve user issues with check out and make the website follow ADA laws and online alcohol sale laws.

Deliverables

A fully responsive website with the same domain as the old website but through a new host.

  • Timeline

    • 4 months - NOV 2024 - FEB 2025

  • My Roles

    • Researcher, Designer, Tester, Photographer

  • Tools

    • WordPress, Adobe Illustrator, Oxygen, HTML, CSS, JS

  • Constraints

    • In terms of cost, scope, and time; cost was the most limiting factor. I was given a lot of freedom in time and scope but was very limited on the plugins and add-ons I could use for the website.

Research

1 The first thing I did was visit as many other winery websites as I could to find what was working and what wasn’t. I took notes on all of these websites and started documenting what I could use vs. what I didn’t think would work to get a better idea of some boundaries for the website.

2 After that I began looking at Pinterest, Dribble, and Behance to figure out what colors, fonts, and aesthetics would work best. I went through tons of pictures, mock ups, and concept projects to find just the right balance of modern and timeless classic.

3 Finally, I created a basic User Flow chart to get an idea of how the website should be laid out. I wanted the main points of interest to be front and center but didn’t want to forget about the less common pages.
